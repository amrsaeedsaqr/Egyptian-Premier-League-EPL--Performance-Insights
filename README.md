1. Executive Summary (The Analyst's View)
  This project involves a comprehensive longitudinal analysis of the Egyptian Premier League spanning 10 seasons. By aggregating data from 2,907 matches and 6,635 goals, the dashboard provides a deep dive into league-wide       performance trends, club dominance, and officiating statistics. The objective was to transform raw match logs into a strategic tool for evaluating team efficiency and stadium impact.

2. Strategic Data Insights
   Scoring Trends & Consistency: The league maintains an average of 2 goals per match. The "Total Goals by Season" trend shows a high degree of scoring stability over the last decade, with a slight dip in the most recent        periods, suggesting evolving defensive tactics across the league.

  Club Dominance: Ahly SC and Zamalek emerge as the primary performance leaders, not only in total points but also in scoring volume (815 and 779 goals respectively).

  Home Field Advantage: Data confirms a notable "Home Advantage" effect, with 36% of matches resulting in Home Wins, significantly outperforming Away Wins (33%) and Ties (31%).

  Officiating & Penalty Dynamics: Out of 6,635 goals, 1,000 were penalties (~15%). The "Penalty Goals by Referee" analysis identifies specific officiating patterns, which is crucial for high-level tactical scouting.

  Venue Utilization: Petrosport Stadium and Borg El Arab are the most utilized venues, hosting over 300+ matches each, and they correlate with the highest aggregate goal counts in the league.

3. Technical Toolkit Highlight
   Relational Data Modeling: Connected multiple disparate datasets (Match Logs, Goal Scorer details, Official/Venue records) using common keys (match_id).

   Trend Forecasting: Used time-series visualization to monitor scoring volatility over a 10-year period.

   Categorical Analysis: Segmented goals by type (Normal, Penalty, Own Goal) and assists by delivery method (Cross, Pass, Free Kick).

   UX Design: Designed an "Overview to Detail" navigation flow, moving from league-wide KPIs to specific referee and stadium deep-dives.
